- Part of me sees static as passive and passive as wrong. Another part of me knows that’s a personal thing - it’s something that indicates something is wrong in my own life, but i’m too quick to judge others.
- Moving has forced so much change that has honestly felt overdue. it’s pushed me to shed some things that i needed to a while back. it’s pushed me to think about how I want to move forward differently. I feel like a snake shedding skin and i feel a bit more glistening and new as a result.
- I’m witness to the amount of effort it takes to go through that crap. And I see how the american suburban three car garage lifestyle contributes to stasis - we can just endlessly cram garbage into every nook and cranny til it’s bursting, and then we cease to change.
- With less space, how can I:
    1. notice that things need to be shed or changes should be made
    2. remove the things that need to go
    3. make changes that push me forward
- How do we make change in our normal lives?
    - by setting aside meaningful time - a hackathon, a game jam, a cabin in the woods to work on a project… we have the vocabulary for projects but I often overlook the upkeep and maintenance for a household as something that merits this dedicated time
    - by making small changes repeatedly - a habit, a lifestyle change, etc. best comes in a package of a little of something over a long period.
- Mixing up what kind of changes can be made in either of these modes can be detrimental
    - Repainting a room is rarely done over time. You need to move all the furniture, cover all the floors, tape up the trim, etc. You’d have to clean the paint supplies every night and put it all back. It’s not worth it to do a little at a time. It’s big enough that you need to focus on doing it all in a single session, and move on. You can’t repaint a house in a day, but you also can’t paint a square foot per day for a week either.
    - Working a new food into your diet doesn’t stick if you try it cold turkey. It requires long term, minute investments into yourself and your diet. You can’t force a change like this by doing nothing but that thing for two weeks.
