I’m stressed about leaving.

I can’t feel the sadness. I don’t know what it feels like. I just feel anxiety for a wall of emotion that I am certain I’ll crash into.

I’m afraid of forgetting. I’m afraid that I didn’t savor something that I should have. I took this all here for granted.

In the eternal now, this is ending. I wish I could live in the now that will be then. I’m scared about what I’ll feel. I’m already begrudging myself for not noticing. How can I learn to savor things better.

I have been thinking about memories more often now. Pictures I wish I would have saved from Japan. Pictures I wish I would have taken in high school. Pictures I wish I would have taken in Berlin.

Important reframing:

I guess there’s something fun about a well of memories that pop up randomly. Like last night when I remembered that Evan showed me Dive Kick during a shooter drill in Otto Miller in 2016 or so. Or the $12 taco box. Maybe can I look at this as a magical transformation? Seattle becomes an old home. San Diego becomes a new one. Both newly special. Additive perhaps?

The ending of something wakes me up. It calls me out of the flow.

I miss our Ballard house. I miss our Berlin apartment. I miss my creepy crawl space in my closet in Poulsbo. I miss my blue window seat in my bedroom. I miss my white chair. I miss the wood stove and the bricks in my nana and papa’s house. I can remember everything.
